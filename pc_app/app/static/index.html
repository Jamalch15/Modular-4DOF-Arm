1. Verify reference frame and tool

Confirm the physical point and Z datum before collecting data. The workflow rejects samples from stale tools, models, or references.

2. Collect fit and held-out validation samples

The app automatically chooses valid joint poses spanning X/Y, height, and tool pitch. This avoids fitting a weak dataset or asking the operator to understand which Z/Phi combinations are informative.

No calibration move previewed.

3. Measure and save sample

Camera X/Y and manual or touch-off Z are noisy measurements. Confirm the marker and coordinate frame before saving.

Manual XY entry is available if vision cannot isolate the TCP marker.

4. Diagnose and fit the physical model

Only identifiable parameter groups are fitted. Updates are proposals until explicitly applied while disarmed.

5. Apply optional residual command correction

Enter measured-minus-commanded reach/Z offsets directly, or fit a correction from saved samples. Positive reach means the TCP lands farther from the base; positive Z means it lands higher.
